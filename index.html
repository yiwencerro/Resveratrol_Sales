DISTRIBUTION ANALYSIS

Distribution Loss, Not Demand Failure SPINS POS

TDP = shelf presence. When TDP falls but $/TDP rises, stores that stock the product sell more of it — the problem is access, not demand. This is key for retailer conversations.

📌 The diagnostic logic: If consumer demand were failing, BOTH TDP and velocity ($/TDP) would decline together. When TDP falls but $/TDP rises — as seen across Reserveage's core SKUs — the product is being delisted from stores but selling well in stores that still carry it. This is a shelf-access problem, not a product problem. SPINS velocity data is the strongest argument for re-listing.

DATA EVIDENCE

Collagen Myth — Complete Analysis NetSuite + SPINS Conditioning

Three separate collagen events: (1) Powder discontinued due to regulations/slow sales. (2) Chews out-of-stock due to supply chain. (3) Neither caused resveratrol decline — resveratrol grew for years after each event.

📌 Collagen context clarified: The "KOR" powder was a Korean-market version discontinued due to regulatory issues. The domestic powder (SKUs 5427/5428/6001) was re-launched but had slow sales and was subsequently discontinued. The Collagen Replenish Chews experienced supply chain out-of-stock issues — a completely separate, operational event, not a strategic discontinuation.

Resveratrol vs Collagen — NetSuite Wholesale Trend NetSuite

✅ Opposite trajectories for 3+ yearsCollagen fell every year while resveratrol grew every year 2020–2023. A halo effect requires synchronized movement. The data shows the opposite.
✅ Powder Halo Analysis — Four Tests
  • ✓
    Powder customers' resveratrol kept growing after powder discontinued. NetSuite: 234 accounts that bought the domestic powder saw resveratrol grow from $1.63M (2020) → $2.33M (2022) → $2.82M (2023) — year after year of growth despite powder being wound down.
  • ✓
    4-year timing gap (powder → resveratrol decline). Domestic powder wound down in 2022. Resveratrol didn't decline until 2024. A 2-year lag destroys the halo hypothesis.
  • ✓
    Collagen chews supply chain issue = operational, not strategic. The chews going out of stock at VS ($57K → $25) was a supply chain problem, not a discontinuation. It removed $57K from VS but had no effect on resveratrol sales (resveratrol grew through the chews' peak period).
  • ✓
    SPINS Conditioning: collagen powder category is actually growing +264% industry-wide. If Reserveage's powder had stayed on shelf, the format was viable. The exit was brand-specific (regulatory/demand), not a format failure.
